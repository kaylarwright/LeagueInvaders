MouseMaze

by: Kayla Wright

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

When I was deciding what kind of I game I should make I was stuck. I had no idea what I wanted to do. So I decided to ask my friend that also did coding if she had any ideas and she suggested a mouse and cat game. Which lead me to build of that idea to create the game I made today.

2. What I Learned

The feedback I got from my teacher was to make the cats(enemies) movements more smoother, keep the mouse in bounds and also make sure the mouse was facing the right way. The feedback I got from a classmate was to fix my instruction page and make sure it was easy for the user to understand where to find it.

3. Architecture of My Game

The different parts of my game is MenuState, instruction pop up, GameState, EndState and WinState. The MenuState tells you the name of the game and shows you how to see the instructions. If you press enter in the MenuState you will go to the GameState yet if you press space in the MenuState you will get the instructions. Once in the GameState the game will start. You will use the arrow keys to dodge the enemies(cats). If a cat does hit you the EndState will immediately show up telling you "GAME OVER". Yet if you make it to the top of the screen in GameState the WinState would immediately show up. The WinState will show you "You Win!!! and your score.

4. Technical Implementation

I can't pick a particular part of the game I am most proud of because I am proud of the whole game. The steps I took to accomplish my goal was reference old code, kept trying even when stuck and asked questions.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of the project was figuring out how to code what I pictured in my head. I knew what I wanted to do yet I did not know how to start making it at first. Yet I started with what I knew about making games from LeagueInvaders.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

An improvement I would like to make if I were to continue working on this game is make my scoring system work a little smoother. How the score works now is when a cat passes the bottom of the screen your score goes up. Yet if you win the game before any of the cats pass the bottom of the screen your score does not move yet you win. So I would like to make the score go up right after you pass the cat not when it passes the bottom.
